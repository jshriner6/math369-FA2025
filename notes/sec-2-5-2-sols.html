    Last time we were introduced to matrices as transformations (functions on vectors). Like functions in one variable, it is often useful to "chain" together transformations (composition).
    ðŸ”—
    Given two transformations \(T: \R^n \to \R^m\text{,}\) \(T(\xvec) = A\xvec\text{,}\) and \(S: \R^m \to \R^p\text{,}\) \(S(\xvec) = B\xvec\text{,}\) we can form the composition transformation
    \begin{equation*} (S \circ T) : \R^n \to \R^p, \,\, (S \circ T)(\xvec) = (BA)\xvec\text{.} \end{equation*}
